The Crisis We Face

Our oceans and consumption patterns are at a tipping point. Understanding the problems is the first step toward meaningful action.

🧴

Plastic Pollution

Over 8 million metric tons of plastic enter our oceans each year. Microplastics have been found in the deepest ocean trenches and in the food we eat.

🌡️

Ocean Warming

The ocean has absorbed over 90% of the excess heat from global warming, causing mass coral bleaching, rising sea levels, and disruption of marine ecosystems.

🎣

Overfishing

Over 34% of the world's fish stocks are fished at unsustainable levels. Destructive fishing practices destroy critical habitats like coral reefs and seagrass beds.

🏭

Overconsumption

Global material consumption has tripled since 1970. Current production and consumption patterns consume far more resources than the Earth can regenerate.

⚗️

Chemical Pollution

Agricultural runoff, industrial waste, and sewage pour nutrients into coastal waters, creating "dead zones" where oxygen is so low that marine life cannot survive.

🪸

Coral Reef Decline

Coral reefs support 25% of all marine life yet cover less than 1% of the ocean floor. Half of shallow water coral reefs have been lost in the last 30 years.

🌱 What Can We Do?
♻️

Reduce, Reuse, Recycle

Cut single-use plastics from your daily life. Choose reusable bags, bottles, and containers. Support brands with sustainable packaging commitments.

🛒

Consume Responsibly

Buy only what you need. Choose sustainably sourced seafood certified by MSC or ASC. Support circular economy businesses that design out waste.

🏖️

Participate in Clean-ups

Join local beach or river clean-up initiatives. Even a single volunteer session removes hundreds of pieces of plastic before they reach the ocean.

📢

Advocate & Educate

Raise awareness among family and friends. Support policies that restrict single-use plastics and strengthen marine protected area legislation.

🌿

Adopt Sustainable Habits

Reduce food waste, compost organic material, and choose plant-rich diets. These actions reduce agricultural runoff and lower your carbon footprint.

Our Mission: This project was created as part of our final year coursework to explore the intersection of Sustainable Development Goals 12 and 14. We believe that education and awareness are the most powerful tools for change. Through interactive learning, we hope to inspire everyone — students, teachers, and community members — to take concrete steps toward a more sustainable future.
